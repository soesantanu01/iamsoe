Santanu Soe

I am a Prime Minister's Research Fellow and Joint PhD Scholar at IIT Madras and the University of Melbourne, specializing in nonsmooth optimization problems. I have received the Melbourne Research Fellowship for my doctoral studies at the University of Melbourne.

My research focuses on improving and analyzing existing algorithms to make them computationally efficient. I work on operator splitting methods, primal-dual algorithms, and monotone inclusion problems, with applications in signal processing, machine learning, and data science.

Operator Splitting Methods

Developing and analyzing efficient algorithms for solving complex optimization problems by decomposing them into simpler subproblems.

Primal-Dual Algorithms

Designing and studying primal-dual methods for saddle point problems and variational inequalities with applications in image processing and machine learning.

Monotone Inclusion Problems

Investigating theory and algorithms for solving monotone inclusion problems, which generalize many optimization problems and variational inequalities.

Computational Optimization

Focusing on developing computationally efficient algorithms with convergence guarantees for large-scale optimization problems.
