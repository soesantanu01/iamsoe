Research Scholar in Optimization & Imaging

Santanu Soe

I am a Prime Minister's Research Fellow and Joint PhD Scholar at IIT Madras and the University of Melbourne, specializing in mathematical optimization for imaging and machine learning.

My research focuses on developing novel algorithms for solving complex optimization problems, with particular emphasis on operator splitting methods, primal-dual algorithms, and structured regularization techniques.

With a strong foundation in functional analysis and convex optimization, I create efficient computational methods that bridge theoretical mathematics with practical applications in medical imaging and large-scale data analysis.

Optimization Algorithms

Developing efficient primal-dual methods with adaptive step-size rules and convergence guarantees for convex-concave saddle point problems.

Imaging Science

Applying advanced regularization techniques to solve ill-posed inverse problems in medical and scientific imaging.

Machine Learning

Creating scalable optimization methods for large-scale machine learning problems with structured regularization.

Research Areas

My research spans several areas of optimization theory with applications in imaging and machine learning.

Primal-Dual & Splitting Algorithms

Developing and analyzing primal-dual algorithms including Golden Ratio Primal-Dual (GRPDA), Forward-Backward, and Condat-Vũ variants with adaptive step-size rules and convergence guarantees.

Operator Splitting Adaptive Steps Convergence Analysis

Imaging & Inverse Problems

Applying total variation (TV) models, robust losses (L1/Huber), and GraphNet regularization to solve denoising, deblurring, and inpainting problems in medical and scientific imaging.

TV / TGV GraphNet Robust Losses

Machine Learning Optimization

Solving sparse logistic regression and composite problems with efficient proximal operators for large-scale machine learning applications.

Composite Models Proximal Operators Large-scale Optimization
